LOW CARBON STEEL

Low carbon steel fixtures are intended for use at temperatures from room temperature up to 500°F (260°C), in normal laboratory humidity environments. Our low carbon steel fixtures are black oxide treated to minimize rust and corrosion in normal laboratory environments. Components requiring high hardness and/or high strength, e.g., specimen grips, buckling constraints, load bearing blocks, etc., are fabricated of heat treated tool steel.

17-4PH STAINLESS STEEL

Our stainless steel fixtures are intended for use at temperatures from cryogenic up to 1000°F (540°C), in either dry or wet environments. All of our stainless steel fixtures are fabricated of 17-4PH stainless steel. The 17-4PH is a high performance stainless steel, having a room temperature yield strength at least twice that of the 300- and 400-series stainless steels typically used, and it retains its strength and stiffness properties much better at elevated temperatures. All components requiring very high hardness and/or yield strength are fabricated of heat treated 440C stainless steel.

NICKEL-BASE SUPERALLOY

All of our fixtures can also be fabricated to order using high temperature nickel-base superalloys. These fixtures can be used at temperatures up to 1500°F (815°C), and in harsh environments.
